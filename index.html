/CREATIVE-RIGHTS

NFTs are important because the rights and laws created around Intellectual Property (IP) will shape the future of digital assets.

They may seem like JPGs today, but tomorrow they will be Virtual Worlds, Artificial Intelligence, and the Internet of Things (IoT).

This is why it is important that we, the creators, lead the way.

We will win the same way wars have been won by the righteous for thousands of years — being stronger together and taking action first.




/NFT-IP

Our #WorldArtDay2021 experiment proved a point. That artists can use their abilities to inspire change in others and that we can rally around a shared belief.

This led me to 3 beliefs about NFT Intellectual Property for creators, of which we have already proven the first, the “right to collect” an NFT:

You need 3 different spaces for NFTs:

1. A space to admire it (collect)

2. A space to make money with it (license)

3. A space to set the rules (own)


NFT IP Use Case Ex. Qty Ex. Price($XTZ) Rights Monetization
Collect Social 500 1 Digital/physical display Royalties on secondary sales
License Marketplace 50 500 Use in non/for-profit ventures Royalties on revenue via ventures
Own Governance 1 50,000 Control "license" and "save" IP One large sum payment

Creators “own” all of the rights when they create an NFT. They can choose how and when to exercise all the rights that come with “ownership.”

Collectors, entrepreneurs, and other stakeholders must be a part of the value network too. We need to provide them with a space to create value and profit off of it in a way that also respects the creator rights while providing them with a passive income stream.

To evolve this new system, we must experiment with the mechanics of it in the real world and use the results to earn the backing and legitimacy of intellectual institutions that can create change (i.e. @CreativeCommons, @ACLU, etc).

Which leads us into our second experiment...




/THIS-WAS-FOR

The murder of Daunte Wright this week and the historic plight of African-Americans in the United States is our battle too. Our battle is for all allies — people that stand up against injustice irrespective of their race, religion, or resume.

On 4/20, a police officer was convicted for the murder of George Floyd, the Black brother whose death sparked an international movement in 2020. A woman standing by the hearing said this:

"This is the first time we’ve convicted a cop in this state for murder, except for the one black cop. This was for George, but this was for a lot of other people too."

This Was For... (thiswasfor.com / thiswasfor.org) is our opportunity as artists, designers, and entrepreneurs to inspire change, take action, and experiment with part 2/3 of NFT IP:




/THE-PLAN

We will create a series of limited edition “This Was For...” Apparel (T-Shirts/Hoodies) and Prints "licensed" from relevant artwork via #WorldArtDay2021 and new #ThisWasFor art created by HEN artists.

The artwork will be related to one of the three themes:

---

This was for...

George Floyd. Breonna Taylor. Daunte Wright... (Racial Rights)

Ross Ulbricht. Aaron Schwartz. Bruce Lee... (Internet Rights)

Ruth Bader Ginsburg. Dorothy Height. Almaas Elman... (Women’s Rights)

---

We will work with designers to create a series of 42 (4/20) Apparel & Print sets for each design and price them at 42 $XTZ ~$230 USD (still /TBD on pricing).

All participating artists will sign a short “contract” that we will create stating they provide thiswasfor.com the “license” to use their NFT/artwork in non-profit/for-profit ventures and in turn will receive 10% royalties on all sales.

^This is how we test the 2nd part of our NFT IP system. That we can create a new way for others to “mix and match” the original NFTs in ventures, while artists still can make money from their creative work.

All “profits” will be donated to Daunte Wright’s GoFundMe

As a participating artist, you can choose if you’d like to keep the 10% royalties or donate it to Daunte’s GoFundMe. No judgement on either, but artists should have the “right” to decide.

(I am reaching out to apparel and print manufacturers in SF/LA to see if we can have the shirts/hoodies/prints donated. If so, this will increase the amount we can donate and help us lower the prices).
